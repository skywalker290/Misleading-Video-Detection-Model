THUGS OF HINDOSTAN (Love: 3/5, an extra 0.5 included for Aamir Khan) If you have huge expectations, managing your expectations will be the key to whether you will like the film or not. • The visual effects, cinematography, presentation and background music make it one of the best movies to come close to any big bonanza Hollywood movie, without a doubt. 1st half is good in most parts, 2nd half is strictly ok.• However, being a fantasy-adventure genre, do not attempt to find logic in the story, and you will be fine, as the screenplay gets a bit lengthy and slow at times, even giving you a ‘deja-vu’ of past Bollywood movies, including the song sequences. The ship sequences are stunning.• If you forgive and forget this, there is a treat waiting in the form of ‘Firangi’, a character which is very well written with dollops of humour and genuinely funny moments, with various plot twists-n-turns (which work to a certain extent). I went with low expectations, but found myself laughing on many occasions and Aamir is just brilliant in this act, something I had not expected. Watch it only for him and on big screen for sure!• Katrina is mainly there for the song-n-dance sequences (although she dances and acts fab, hers is more of a Guest-appearance and her fans will be disappointed, as there should have been more of her), Amitabh is a treat to watch and Fatima’s performance is good but functional. Zeeshan Ayub was good, although we wanted more.• The actions sequences are good however get repetitive, music sounds jaded and faded, screenplay lengthy (people took breaks during songs), non-existent storyline and direction should have been more ‘spot-on’ given the scale (hence budget) of film, credibility of having a stellar cast and due to the fact that YRF backing this film.It is a movie that “could” have been a game-changer. Inspite of the criticism, one must give credit to the makers to attempt something grand in Bollywood and the effort is to be lauded. It is a one-time watch and does entertain in many parts !
